    <h1>Video Game Studies: Core Concepts Assignment</h1>

    <h2>Section 1: History and Chronology</h2>

    <div class="question">Question 1: The Crash and The Revival</div>
    <div class="answer">
        <ul>
            <li><strong>1983 Video Game Crash:</strong> Market oversaturation, poor-quality games, declining consumer confidence, overproduction. Notorious example: <em>E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial</em>.</li>
            <li><strong>Reviving Company & Console:</strong> Nintendo – NES (Nintendo Entertainment System)</li>
            <li><strong>Famous Mascot:</strong> Mario</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="question">Question 2: Era Differentiation</div>
    <div class="answer">
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Golden Age of Arcades (1970–1983):</strong> Key Game: <em>Pac-Man</em>; Social Setting: Public (arcades)</li>
            <li><strong>Modern 3D & Online Era (2000–2010):</strong> Key Game: <em>World of Warcraft</em>; Social Setting: Private/Online (home)</li>
            <li><strong>Fundamental Difference:</strong> Public social gameplay vs private/online social interaction</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="question">Question 3: The 16-bit Wars</div>
    <div class="answer">
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Main Companies & Consoles:</strong> Sega – Genesis/Mega Drive, Nintendo – SNES</li>
            <li><strong>Pivotal PC Games:</strong> <em>Doom</em> (FPS), <em>Civilization</em> (Turn-based strategy)</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <h2>Section 2: Genre Classification & Evolution</h2>

    <div class="question">Question 4: Genre Definition and Examples</div>
    <div class="answer">
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Metroidvania:</strong> Exploration-focused 2D/2.5D platforming with interconnected maps and ability-based progression. <span class="example">Example: Castlevania: Symphony of the Night</span></li>
            <li><strong>JRPG:</strong> Story-driven RPG with turn-based combat and narrative emphasis. <span class="example">Example: Final Fantasy VI</span></li>
            <li><strong>RTS:</strong> Real-time resource management, unit building, and combat. <span class="example">Example: StarCraft</span></li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="question">Question 5: Understanding Hybridization</div>
    <div class="answer">
        Roguelike (e.g., Nethack) features procedural generation and permadeath. Roguelite (e.g., Hades) maintains procedural levels but allows persistent progression between deaths, reducing penalty. Key difference: Meta-progression.
    </div>

    <div class="question">Question 6: The Souls-like Subgenre</div>
    <div class="answer">
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Defining Title:</strong> Demon's Souls</li>
            <li><strong>Core Design Elements:</strong> High combat difficulty, interconnected world design with environmental storytelling</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <h2>Section 3: Influence and Key Titles</h2>

    <div class="question">Question 7: Technology as a Driver</div>
    <div class="answer">
        3D graphics enabled fully navigable environments and player freedom, enabling Open-World games. <strong>Key Title:</strong> Grand Theft Auto III
    </div>

    <div class="question">Question 8: Community and Modding</div>
    <div class="answer">
        Modding communities can create new genres. Example: MOBA genre originated from a mod of <em>Warcraft III</em> called DotA.
    </div>

    <div class="question">Question 9: The Pillars of Gaming</div>
    <div class="answer">
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Tetris:</strong> Skill-focused, precise gameplay loops</li>
            <li><strong>The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind:</strong> Narrative-driven, exploration and scale</li>
            <li><strong>Contribution Difference:</strong> Tetris influenced addictive mechanics; Morrowind influenced open-world exploration and narrative freedom</li>
        </ul>
    </div>
